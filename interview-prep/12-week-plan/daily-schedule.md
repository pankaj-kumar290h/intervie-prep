# Daily Schedule Template

A practical guide for managing your interview prep time around a full-time job.

---

## Time Management Principles

1. **Consistency > Intensity:** 1 hour daily beats 7 hours once a week
2. **Quality > Quantity:** Focus on understanding, not just solving
3. **Spaced Repetition:** Review previous topics weekly
4. **Active Learning:** Code, don't just read

---

## Daily Schedule Templates

### Weekday Schedule (1 hour)

#### Option A: Morning Person
```
6:00 AM - 6:30 AM: Study new concept (30 min)
  - Read study materials
  - Take notes
  - Understand concepts

6:30 AM - 7:00 AM: Solve problems (30 min)
  - 1-2 LeetCode problems
  - Focus on understanding, not speed
```

#### Option B: Evening Person
```
7:00 PM - 7:30 PM: Study new concept (30 min)
  - Read study materials
  - Watch video explanations if needed

7:30 PM - 8:00 PM: Solve problems (30 min)
  - 1-2 LeetCode problems
  - Practice explaining solutions
```

#### Option C: Lunch Break
```
12:30 PM - 1:00 PM: Review notes (15 min)
  - Quick review of previous day

1:00 PM - 1:30 PM: Solve one problem (30 min)
  - Focus on one problem
  - Understand the pattern
```

### Weekend Schedule (2-3 hours)

#### Saturday
```
9:00 AM - 10:00 AM: Deep dive into topic (1 hour)
  - Study complex concepts
  - Implement data structures from scratch
  - Review week's progress

10:00 AM - 11:30 AM: Practice problems (1.5 hours)
  - Solve 2-3 problems
  - Focus on Medium difficulty
  - Time yourself

11:30 AM - 12:00 PM: Review and reflect (30 min)
  - Review solutions
  - Identify patterns
  - Note weak areas
```

#### Sunday
```
10:00 AM - 11:00 AM: Review week's topics (1 hour)
  - Review all problems from the week
  - Re-solve 1-2 problems
  - Review notes

11:00 AM - 12:00 PM: Practice problems (1 hour)
  - Solve new problems
  - Focus on weak areas

12:00 PM - 12:30 PM: Plan next week (30 min)
  - Review next week's plan
  - Set goals
  - Prepare study materials
```

## Weekly Time Breakdown

### Total: 7-8 hours/week

- **Weekdays (5 days):** 1 hour/day = 5 hours
- **Weekend (2 days):** 1.5 hours/day = 3 hours
- **Total:** 8 hours

### Distribution:
- **Study:** 30-40% (2.5-3 hours)
- **Practice:** 50-60% (4-5 hours)
- **Review:** 10-20% (1 hour)

## Productivity Tips

### Create a Study Environment
- **Dedicated space:** Same place every day
- **Minimize distractions:** Phone on silent
- **Have materials ready:** Notes, laptop, water

### Use Pomodoro Technique
- **25 min:** Focused study/practice
- **5 min:** Break
- **Repeat:** 2-3 cycles per session

### Track Your Time
- **Use timer:** Know how long tasks take
- **Log hours:** Track weekly progress
- **Adjust:** If behind, prioritize practice

---

## Handling Busy Weeks

### If You're Behind
1. **Prioritize practice:** Solving > reading
2. **Focus on patterns:** Don't try to cover everything
3. **Extend timeline:** It's okay to take longer
4. **Quality over quantity:** Better to master 3 problems than rush through 10

### If You're Ahead
1. **Deepen understanding:** Review optimal solutions
2. **Practice explaining:** Record yourself explaining
3. **Tackle Hard problems:** Challenge yourself
4. **Help others:** Teaching reinforces learning

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: "I don't have time"
**Solution:**
- Start with 30 min/day
- Use commute time
- Wake up 30 min earlier
- Reduce social media time

### Challenge: "I'm too tired after work"
**Solution:**
- Study in the morning
- Use lunch break
- Study on weekends
- Take breaks when needed

### Challenge: "I'm not making progress"
**Solution:**
- Review your approach
- Focus on understanding, not speed
- Get help (peers, online communities)
- Take a break and come back fresh

### Challenge: "Problems are too hard"
**Solution:**
- Start with Easy problems
- Read solutions after trying
- Focus on patterns, not individual problems
- Practice more of the same type
